Hotel Rate Plans Explained: EP, CP, MAP, AP Full Forms & Meanings

By Hotel Playbook Team Updated: February 2026 10 min read

When booking a hotel in India or anywhere in the world, you'll come across terms like EP, CP, MAP, and AP. These are meal plan codes that tell you exactly what's included in your room rate. Understanding these plans helps you compare prices accurately and avoid surprises at checkout.

Whether you're a traveler trying to understand your booking or a hotelier setting up rate plans, this guide covers everything you need to know about hotel meal plans.

Quick Summary: EP = Room Only, CP = Room + Breakfast, MAP = Room + Breakfast + Dinner, AP = Room + All Meals

What is a Hotel Rate Plan?

A rate plan (also called a meal plan) defines what's included in your hotel room price beyond just the accommodation. The main difference between plans is the number of meals included.

Hotels use these standardized codes so guests can easily compare offerings across different properties. The same codes are used worldwide, though some regions prefer alternative names.

The Four Main Hotel Rate Plans

EP - European Plan

Room Only (No Meals)

Just the room, no meals included. Most flexible and usually cheapest option.

Also called: RO (Room Only)

CP - Continental Plan

Room + Breakfast

Room with complimentary breakfast. Most popular choice for business and leisure travelers.

Also called: BB (Bed & Breakfast)

MAP - Modified American Plan

Room + Breakfast + Dinner

Two meals included. Ideal for resorts and vacation stays where you're out during the day.

Also called: HB (Half Board)

AP - American Plan

Room + All Meals

All three meals included. Best for all-inclusive resorts and remote locations.

Also called: FB (Full Board)

Complete Comparison: EP vs CP vs MAP vs AP

Plan Full Form Meals Included Best For Price Range
EP European Plan None Budget travelers, food explorers Lowest
CP Continental Plan Breakfast Business trips, short stays Low-Medium
MAP Modified American Plan Breakfast + Dinner Resorts, family vacations Medium-High
AP American Plan All 3 Meals All-inclusive stays, remote hotels Highest

Indian Variants: EPAI, CPAI, MAPAI, APAI

In India, you'll often see rate plans with an "AI" or "I" suffix. This stands for "All Indian" or simply "Indian", indicating that the meals are Indian cuisine (typically a buffet with North and South Indian options).

Plan Meaning What's Different
EPAI European Plan All Indian Room only (same as EP)
CPAI Continental Plan All Indian Indian breakfast buffet (idli, dosa, paratha, poha, etc.)
MAPAI Modified American Plan All Indian Indian breakfast + Indian dinner buffet
APAI American Plan All Indian All Indian meals throughout the day
Tip for Travelers: If you prefer continental or international cuisine, look for plans without the "AI" suffix, or check with the hotel about their menu options.

International Equivalents

When booking international hotels (especially in Europe), you might see different terms for the same concepts:

Which Rate Plan Should You Choose?

Choose EP (Room Only) If:

  • You want to explore local restaurants and street food
  • You're on a tight budget and prefer eating outside
  • You have specific dietary requirements not easily met by hotel buffets
  • You're staying in a city with great food options nearby

Choose CP (Breakfast Included) If:

  • You want a hassle-free start to your day
  • You're on a business trip with morning meetings
  • You're traveling with family and want convenience
  • You prefer flexibility for lunch and dinner plans

Choose MAP (Breakfast + Dinner) If:

  • You're staying at a resort or hill station
  • Limited dining options exist near the hotel
  • You want to relax without worrying about dinner plans
  • You're on a family vacation and want predictable meal times

Choose AP (All Meals) If:

  • You're at an all-inclusive resort
  • The hotel is in a remote location with no nearby restaurants
  • You want complete relaxation without leaving the property
  • You're traveling with kids and prefer set meal times

Tips for Hoteliers: Setting Up Rate Plans

Revenue Manager's Perspective: Here's how to optimize your meal plan offerings.

Best Practices:

  1. Always offer EP as base: Let guests add meals if they want. This gives you the lowest visible price for OTA comparisons.
  2. Price meals attractively in bundles: If breakfast costs ₹500 walk-in, offer CP at ₹400 extra to incentivize booking with meals.
  3. Use MAP for resort properties: It encourages on-property dining and increases F&B revenue.
  4. Seasonal adjustments: During peak season, push AP/MAP plans. During lean season, focus on EP to attract price-sensitive guests.
  5. Clear naming on OTAs: Use full descriptions like "Room Only (EP)" rather than just codes to avoid guest confusion.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does EP plan in hotel mean?
EP stands for European Plan. It means you're booking only the room with no meals included. You pay separately for any food you order at the hotel restaurant, or you can eat outside.
What is the difference between CP and MAP?
CP (Continental Plan) includes only breakfast with your room. MAP (Modified American Plan) includes both breakfast and dinner. MAP is better for resort stays where you want two meals sorted, while CP is ideal for city hotels where you want flexibility for other meals.
What does MAPAI mean in hotel booking?
MAPAI stands for "Modified American Plan All Indian." It means your room comes with Indian-style breakfast and dinner buffets. This is common in Indian hotels and typically includes dishes like dal, rice, rotis, sabzi, and regional specialties.
Is CP same as bed and breakfast?
Yes, CP (Continental Plan) and BB (Bed & Breakfast) are essentially the same - both include room accommodation plus breakfast. "BB" is more commonly used in European and international hotels, while "CP" is the standard term in India.
Which meal plan is cheapest?
EP (European Plan / Room Only) is always the cheapest as it includes no meals. However, if you plan to eat at the hotel anyway, CP or MAP can offer better value as bundled meals are usually discounted compared to walk-in restaurant prices.
Can I upgrade my meal plan after booking?
Most hotels allow you to upgrade your meal plan at check-in or during your stay. However, you may not get the same discounted rate as booking the plan upfront. It's best to decide your meal preference while booking.
What meals are included in AP plan?
AP (American Plan) includes all three meals: breakfast, lunch, and dinner. Some hotels also include afternoon tea or evening snacks. This is the most comprehensive meal plan and is common at all-inclusive resorts.
What is the full form of MAP in hotel?
MAP stands for Modified American Plan. It includes room accommodation with breakfast and dinner (two meals). It's called "modified" because the original American Plan (AP) includes all three meals - MAP is a modification with one less meal.
