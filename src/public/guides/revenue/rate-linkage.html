Rate Linkage Explained: How to Manage Multiple Rate Plans Easily

By OnlineHotelier Insights Team Updated: February 2026 8 min read

Many small hotels avoid offering multiple rate plans because it seems too complicated to manage. But what if you could set one base rate and have all your other rates automatically calculated? That's exactly what rate linkage does — and it's available on almost every major OTA.

Quick Summary: Rate linkage (also called derived rates or child rates) lets you define one master rate plan. All your other rate plans — refundable, non-refundable, weekly packages, meal-inclusive plans — are automatically calculated based on percentage adjustments you set once. Update one rate, and all linked rates update instantly.

What is Rate Linkage?

Rate linkage is a feature that automatically calculates rates for secondary rate plans based on a primary (master) rate plan.

Instead of manually entering rates for every single rate plan across every single date, you:

  1. Set up your base rate plan (usually your standard flexible rate)
  2. Create linked rate plans with percentage adjustments
  3. Update just your base rate — all other rates adjust automatically

Different OTAs call this feature by different names, but the concept is identical:

OTA Platform Feature Name Where to Find It
Booking.com Derived Rates Extranet → Rates & Availability → Rate Plans
Agoda Child Rates YCS Dashboard → Rate Management
MakeMyTrip Rate Linkage Extranet → Rate Plans
Goibibo Linked Rates Hotel Dashboard → Pricing

Why Should You Use Rate Linkage?

Rate linkage solves three major problems for hotel owners:

1. Saves Massive Time

Instead of updating 5-10 different rate plans every time demand changes, you update one base rate. The rest happen automatically. A task that used to take 30 minutes now takes 3 minutes.

2. Prevents Pricing Errors

Manually managing multiple rates leads to mistakes: non-refundable rates accidentally set higher than flexible rates, breakfast-inclusive rates cheaper than room-only, inconsistent discounts across dates. Rate linkage eliminates these errors.

3. Encourages Better Rate Strategy

Many hotels only offer one rate plan because managing multiple plans feels overwhelming. With rate linkage, you can confidently offer refundable, non-refundable, advance purchase, weekly rates, and meal plans — giving guests more options without extra work.

Real Impact: Hotels offering 3-4 rate plans typically see 8-12% higher revenue than hotels with just one rate plan. Non-refundable rates alone can capture 15-20% more revenue from price-sensitive guests booking in advance.

How Rate Linkage Works: A Practical Example

Example: 15-Room Boutique Hotel

Step 1: You set your Flexible Rate (master rate) at ₹3,000 per night.

Step 2: You create linked rate plans with these adjustments:

  • Non-Refundable Rate: -10% from master = ₹2,700
  • Breakfast Inclusive: +15% from master = ₹3,450
  • Weekly Stay (7+ nights): -20% from master = ₹2,400

Step 3: You raise your base rate to ₹3,500 for a festival weekend.

Automatic Result:
• Flexible Rate: ₹3,500
• Non-Refundable: ₹3,150 (automatically adjusted)
• Breakfast Inclusive: ₹4,025 (automatically adjusted)
• Weekly Stay: ₹2,800 (automatically adjusted)

You changed one number. Four rates updated correctly across all channels. Zero manual work. Zero errors.

Common Rate Plans to Link

Here are the most effective rate plans to set up using rate linkage:

1. Non-Refundable Rate

Discount: -10% to -15% from base rate
Why it works: Guests booking in advance are willing to commit in exchange for savings. You get guaranteed revenue.

2. Advance Purchase Rates

Discount: -15% to -25% for bookings 15-30 days in advance
Why it works: Encourages early bookings, helps forecast demand, locks in revenue.

3. Meal-Inclusive Plans

Markup: +10% to +20% for breakfast, +30% to +50% for MAP/AP
Why it works: Increases total revenue per booking, enhances guest convenience.

4. Extended Stay Discounts

Discount: -15% to -25% for stays of 5+ or 7+ nights
Why it works: Fills rooms during low-occupancy periods, reduces per-night operational costs.

5. Last-Minute Deals

Discount: -20% to -30% for same-day or next-day bookings
Why it works: Sells inventory that would otherwise go unsold, better than ₹0 revenue.

How to Set Up Rate Linkage (Step-by-Step)

On Booking.com

  1. Log into your Extranet
  2. Go to Rates & Availability → Rate Plans
  3. Create a new rate plan and select "Derived Rate"
  4. Choose your master rate plan (e.g., Flexible Rate)
  5. Set the percentage adjustment (e.g., -10% for non-refundable)
  6. Define cancellation policy and other conditions
  7. Save — the derived rate now updates automatically

On Agoda

  1. Log into YCS (Your Control Center)
  2. Navigate to Rate Management
  3. Select "Create Child Rate"
  4. Link to your parent rate plan
  5. Set percentage or fixed amount adjustment
  6. Configure restrictions (min nights, advance booking, etc.)
  7. Activate — child rate syncs with parent automatically

On MakeMyTrip

  1. Access your hotel Extranet
  2. Go to Rate Plans section
  3. Click "Add Linked Rate"
  4. Select the base rate plan
  5. Enter your rate linkage percentage
  6. Set booking conditions
  7. Submit — linked rate calculates automatically
Pro Tip: If you use a Channel Manager (like eZee, RMS Cloud, SiteMinder), you can set up rate linkage once in your PMS, and it will sync to all OTAs automatically. This is the most efficient way to manage rates across 5-10 channels.

Best Practices for Rate Linkage

✅ Do This:

  • Start simple: Begin with just 2-3 linked rates (flexible, non-refundable, breakfast-inclusive)
  • Test your percentages: Run rates for 2-4 weeks, then adjust based on booking patterns
  • Make non-refundable rates attractive: -10% to -15% discount is the sweet spot
  • Review quarterly: Adjust your linkage percentages seasonally if needed
  • Keep it logical: Non-refundable should always be cheaper than flexible; meal plans should always cost more than room-only

❌ Don't Do This:

  • Don't over-complicate: 8-10 rate plans is overkill; 3-5 is ideal for most hotels
  • Don't set tiny discounts: A -3% non-refundable discount won't motivate anyone; aim for -10% minimum
  • Don't forget restrictions: Your 7-night stay rate should require a 7-night minimum booking
  • Don't link everything: Special promotional rates should be standalone, not linked

When NOT to Use Rate Linkage

Rate linkage is powerful, but there are scenarios where manual rates work better:

  • Flash sales or limited-time promotions: These require fixed rates, not percentage-based adjustments
  • Corporate contracts: Negotiated rates are usually fixed amounts, not percentages
  • Event-specific pricing: Wedding blocks or conference rates often need custom pricing
  • Dynamic pricing experiments: If you're testing aggressive pricing changes on one plan only

The Bottom Line

Rate linkage is one of the simplest ways to increase revenue without increasing workload. It lets small hotels compete with larger properties by offering the same variety of rate plans — refundable, non-refundable, advance purchase, meal-inclusive — without spending hours updating prices.

If you're currently managing multiple rate plans manually, or if you've avoided creating multiple rate plans because it seems too complex, rate linkage solves both problems.

Next Steps:
  1. Log into your OTA extranet (Booking.com, Agoda, or MakeMyTrip)
  2. Set up one derived/child/linked rate as a test (start with non-refundable at -10%)
  3. Monitor bookings for 2-3 weeks
  4. Add 1-2 more linked rates once you're comfortable

Spending 30 minutes setting up rate linkage today will save you hours every week — and likely increase your revenue in the process.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is rate linkage in hotels?

Rate linkage is a feature that automatically calculates rates for secondary rate plans based on a primary (master) rate plan. Instead of manually entering rates for every rate plan, you set up a base rate plan and create linked rate plans with percentage adjustments. When you update your base rate, all linked rates adjust automatically.

What are derived rates on Booking.com?

Derived rates on Booking.com are secondary rate plans that automatically calculate based on a master rate plan. For example, if your flexible rate is ₹3,000 and you set a non-refundable derived rate at -10%, it will automatically be ₹2,700. When you change your master rate, all derived rates update automatically.

What are child rates on Agoda?

Child rates on Agoda are rate plans linked to a parent rate plan. They work the same as derived rates on Booking.com. You define a percentage or fixed amount adjustment, and the child rate automatically calculates based on the parent rate. This saves time managing multiple rate plans.

How do I set up rate linkage on MakeMyTrip?

To set up rate linkage on MakeMyTrip: 1) Log into your hotel Extranet, 2) Go to Rate Plans section, 3) Click "Add Linked Rate", 4) Select your base rate plan, 5) Enter your rate linkage percentage (e.g., -10% for non-refundable), 6) Set booking conditions, 7) Submit. The linked rate will now calculate automatically.

What percentage discount should I offer on non-refundable rates?

The ideal discount for non-refundable rates is 10-15% off your flexible rate. This is enough to motivate guests to commit to a non-refundable booking, while still maintaining healthy margins. Discounts below 5% are too small to be attractive, while discounts above 20% may unnecessarily reduce revenue.

Can I use rate linkage with a channel manager?

Yes, if you use a channel manager (like eZee, RMS Cloud, or SiteMinder), you can set up rate linkage once in your PMS, and it will sync to all OTAs automatically. This is the most efficient way to manage linked rates across 5-10 channels simultaneously.
