GDS: Global Distribution System Explained

By Hotel Playbook Team 5 min read

What is GDS?

GDS = Global Distribution System

A network that connects hotels to travel agents and corporate booking tools worldwide. Think of it as a B2B channel for hotel bookings.

How GDS Works

  1. Hotel connects to GDS: You list your hotel on GDS networks
  2. Travel agent searches: Agent uses GDS terminal to find hotels
  3. Agent books for client: Corporate client's travel desk makes reservation
  4. Booking confirmed: Reservation flows to your PMS
  5. Commission charged: 10-15% typically

Major GDS Providers

Does Your Hotel Need GDS?

GDS is Valuable If:
  • You target corporate/business travelers
  • You're in a business district or near airports
  • You want international travel agent bookings
  • You're a 4-5 star property
GDS May Not Be Worth It If:
  • You're a budget/economy hotel
  • Your guests are primarily leisure travelers
  • You're in a tourist-only destination
  • You have less than 30 rooms

Where GDS Bookings Come From

  • Corporate travel desks: Companies book hotels for employees
  • Travel Management Companies (TMCs): Like SOTC, Thomas Cook, FCM
  • Traditional travel agents: Still use GDS for hotel bookings
  • Consortia: Groups of travel agents (Virtuoso, Signature)
  • Corporate booking tools: Concur, SAP Concur, TripActions

How to Connect to GDS

  1. Direct connection: Contract directly with Amadeus, Sabre, Travelport (expensive, for big chains)
  2. Via Switch company: Use intermediaries like Derbysoft, SiteMinder, or your Channel Manager
  3. Via representation company: Use services like Pegasus, Synxis

GDS Codes for Your Hotel

Once connected, your hotel gets unique codes:

  • Chain Code: Identifies your brand (e.g., "HI" for Holiday Inn)
  • Property Code: Unique identifier for your specific hotel
  • Rate Codes: Different rates for different corporate accounts
