Who This Guide is For

This guide covers two property types that share a similar distribution strategy:

  • Standard Hotels (Budget to Luxury): Traditional 1-star to 5-star properties with standard room categories, front desk, and structured operations
  • Boutique Hotels: Smaller, design-led properties (typically 10-50 rooms) with unique character, personalized service, and a distinct identity

Both property types serve a mix of domestic and international travelers, need broad OTA distribution, and benefit from the same core platforms.

1. MakeMyTrip / Goibibo

Why It's Essential

MakeMyTrip (which also owns Goibibo) is the undisputed leader in Indian domestic hotel bookings. Listing on one automatically makes you visible on both platforms.

  • Market share: ~60% of online hotel bookings in India
  • Commission: 15-25% (varies by city and property category)
  • Payment: Guest pays MakeMyTrip, weekly settlement to hotel
  • Best for: All hotel types, especially domestic travelers

Tips for Hotels

  • Participate in MMT Select or Goibibo Top Rated programs for higher visibility
  • Use "Sell Tonight" deals for last-minute inventory
  • Keep your extranet content updated — fresh photos boost conversion by 20-30%

Tips for Boutique Hotels

  • Highlight your unique selling points in the property description
  • Upload lifestyle photos, not just room shots — show the experience
  • Use the "Boutique" or "Heritage" tags in your property type settings

2. Booking.com

Why It's Essential

Booking.com is the global leader with 28+ million listings worldwide. It's the #1 platform for international guests traveling to India. Read our Booking.com listing guide for pay-at-hotel and no-show tips.

  • Global reach: Travelers from 200+ countries
  • Commission: 15-20%
  • Payment: Guest can pay at hotel (pay-at-property) or online
  • Best for: International guests, boutique hotels especially

Why Boutique Hotels Excel Here

Booking.com has a strong "boutique hotel" search filter that international travelers use. Properties with unique design and character naturally rank well in this segment. Their Genius loyalty program also drives repeat bookings.

Key Insight: Booking.com's "pay at property" model means you collect payment directly from the guest and pay commission on invoice. This is better for cash flow compared to OTAs that collect payment and settle later.

3. Agoda

Why It Matters

Agoda (part of Booking Holdings, same parent as Booking.com) is the go-to platform for travelers from Southeast Asia, China, Japan, and Korea.

  • Strength: Asia-Pacific traveler market
  • Commission: 15-22%
  • Payment: Guest pays Agoda, monthly settlement
  • Best for: Hotels in tourist destinations visited by Asian travelers (Goa, Rajasthan, Kerala, Delhi)

When to Prioritize Agoda

  • Your city has direct flights from Bangkok, Singapore, Kuala Lumpur, or Tokyo
  • You see Asian travelers in your area but aren't capturing them
  • You're in a leisure destination popular with international tourists

4. Cleartrip

Why It Matters

Cleartrip, now owned by Flipkart, is growing rapidly in India's hotel booking space. Its integration with Flipkart's massive user base gives it unique reach.

  • Strength: Growing domestic platform with Flipkart integration
  • Commission: 15-20%
  • Payment: Guest pays Cleartrip, weekly settlement
  • Best for: Domestic leisure and increasingly corporate

Why Include Cleartrip

  • Flipkart users get hotel deals, bringing a new customer segment
  • Lower competition than MakeMyTrip (fewer hotels = better visibility for you)
  • Growing corporate travel segment with SuperCoins integration

5. International Pick (Choose Based on Your Market)

Your 5th OTA should match the international traveler profile in your location:

Pro Tip: Check your Google Analytics or PMS data to see which countries your website visitors and past guests come from. This tells you exactly which international OTA to prioritize.

Setting Up: What You Need

  1. Property photos: Minimum 20 high-quality images (rooms, lobby, exterior, dining, amenities)
  2. Room categories: Define your room types with accurate descriptions and max occupancy
  3. Rate plans: Set up EP, CP, MAP as needed (see our Rate Plans guide)
  4. Cancellation policy: Define free cancellation, non-refundable, and partial refund options
  5. Channel Manager: Use a channel manager to sync rates across all OTAs automatically

Boutique Hotel Specific Strategy

Boutique hotels have a unique advantage on OTAs — they stand out from cookie-cutter chain properties. Maximize this:

  • Tell your story: Why does your property exist? What makes it special? OTA descriptions that tell a story convert better
  • Curate your photos: Show the design details, local art, unique spaces — not just standard room angles
  • Price for value: Boutique travelers pay more for experience. Don't compete on price with budget hotels
  • Respond to every review: Boutique guests care deeply about personal touch — your review responses prove it
  • Leverage Airbnb: Many boutique hotels also list on Airbnb as a "Boutique Hotel" to capture experience-seeking travelers
