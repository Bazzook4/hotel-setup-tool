How Indian Hotels Can Attract International Guests: The Right OTA for Every Nationality, and How to Boost Your Visibility on It

By OnlineHotelier Insights Team Updated July 2026 9 min read

Quick Answer

International guests book on different OTAs than Indian travellers.

American tourists use Expedia. European and Israeli guests prefer Booking.com. Southeast Asian travellers use Agoda. To attract them, you need two things: (1) a listing on the OTA they actually use, and (2) a visibility boost on that OTA targeting their home country.

The booking that never came

Vikram runs a 16-room guesthouse in Kasol, Himachal Pradesh. Every May and October, he sees groups of Israeli backpackers walking past his property. They stop at the dhaba next door, ask the owner for recommendations. The dhaba owner gives three names. None of them Vikram's.

Vikram is on MakeMyTrip. He even has Booking.com. But he never gets these bookings.

Six months later, at a hotelier meetup in Manali, he meets Priya. She runs a smaller 10-room property two lanes away. She says 40% of her bookings in peak season are from Israel. She is booked out. Same location. Same type of property. Very different outcome.

The difference is not the property. It is visibility. Priya had activated Booking.com's Visibility Booster specifically targeting Israel. Vikram had not. That one setting, on one platform, was the gap between full occupancy and empty rooms.

This guide tells you exactly what Priya did, and how to apply it to your own property based on your location and target nationality.

Why international guests are worth chasing

Before you invest time setting up new OTA accounts, it helps to know what is at stake. The gap between a domestic booking and an international booking is not marginal.

  • ADR (Average Daily Rate): Domestic guests on MakeMyTrip (MMT) typically pay ₹2,500 to ₹4,500 per night in the budget to mid-scale segment. International tourists booking the same room on Booking.com pay ₹4,500 to ₹9,000 per night.
  • Booking lead time: International guests book 30 to 60 days in advance, versus 3 to 7 days for domestic travellers. That gives you predictable occupancy and better cash flow planning.
  • Average stay length: International guests stay 3 to 5 nights on average. Domestic guests stay 1.5 to 2 nights. Longer stays mean lower cleaning and check-in costs per rupee earned.
  • Review quality: International guests leave detailed English reviews. These reviews drive organic search visibility on Google and improve your OTA ranking with international traveller segments.
  • Word-of-mouth value: Israeli, German, and UK traveller communities are tight-knit. One good experience leads to referrals through forums, WhatsApp groups, and travel blogs. The repeat guest probability is lower, but the referral chain can be long.
The revenue difference in numbers: A hotel in Goa with 30 rooms that shifts even 5 rooms from domestic ₹3,500 per night guests to international ₹7,000 per night guests earns an extra ₹10.5 lakh per month at 100% occupancy on those rooms. That is ₹1.26 crore per year from one listing change.

How to find out which nationalities visit your destination

Before you start listing on new OTAs, find out which nationalities are most likely to visit your specific location. This saves you from investing in the wrong platform. Here is how.

  1. Check your C-Form and GRC register. Every Indian hotel is legally required to collect Form C (the C-Form) from all foreign national guests. The GRC (Guest Registration Card) holds the same data for all guests. Look at the last 12 months of C-Form entries and count which countries appear most. That is your answer. If you have not been filing Form C consistently, this is also a good reminder that non-compliance carries fine risk.
  2. Your Booking.com extranet. Inside your Booking.com partner dashboard, go to Analytics and then Booking by market. This shows you which countries your bookings are coming from. Even a handful of international bookings will tell you which markets are finding you organically.
  3. State tourism board data. State tourism boards publish annual reports with nationality breakdowns. Himachal Pradesh Tourism, Goa Tourism Board, Rajasthan Tourism, and Kerala Tourism all publish inbound tourist nationality data. Search for "[state] tourism annual report 2024 nationality" to find it.
  4. Ask your local tourism cluster. Other hoteliers in your area often know this better than any report. The dhaba owner in Kasol knows which nationalities pass through Parvati Valley every season. Local knowledge is frequently more accurate than state statistics, which lag by 12 to 18 months.
  5. Google Analytics on your hotel website. If your hotel has a direct booking website with Google Analytics (GA4) installed, check the Audience reports for visitor country data. This shows which nationalities are already searching for your property online.

Step 1: List on the OTA your target nationality uses

Most Indian hotels stop at Booking.com and MakeMyTrip. For international guests, that covers Europe, Israel, and domestic India well. But it misses the US and Canada market almost entirely, and it underserves the fast-growing Southeast Asian segment. For a full comparison of all major OTAs for Indian hotels, see our guide: Best OTA for Indian hotels: comparing the top 10 platforms.

Expedia (for US, Canada, and Australian guests)

Sign up at Expedia Partner Central (partners.expediagroup.com). The process takes 5 to 7 business days. Commission is 15 to 25% depending on your property type and market. One listing covers Expedia.com, Hotels.com, Vrbo (for villa properties), and Orbitz. That is four platforms from one account.

Agoda (for Southeast Asian and Korean guests)

Sign up at ycs.agoda.com (YCS stands for Your Connectivity Solutions, Agoda's partner portal). Onboarding is fast: typically 2 to 3 business days. Commission is 15 to 18% for most Indian properties. Agoda has particularly strong reach in Singapore, Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, and South Korea. If your property is in Andaman, Goa, or Kerala, Agoda is your best channel for Southeast Asian travellers.

Trip.com (for Korean and Japanese guests)

Sign up at trip.com/hotels/supplier or connect via a connectivity partner. Onboarding takes 7 to 14 days and requires standard property documentation. Trip.com works best for properties near India's major heritage and spiritual sites: Agra, Jaipur, Varanasi, Rishikesh. Korean and Japanese tour groups follow these routes consistently.

One important note about channel managers: When you list on 3 to 4 OTAs simultaneously, managing room inventory manually becomes risky. If you sell the same room on Booking.com and Expedia at the same time, you get a double booking. A channel manager (software that syncs your room availability across all OTAs at once) connects all your OTAs to one inventory pool and updates them in real time. Step 2: Use geo-boost features to appear at the top for international travellers

Being listed is not enough. In popular destinations like Goa, there are 400+ hotels on Booking.com. Your listing may appear on page 8. A traveller from Amsterdam searching for Goa in January never sees you. This is where geo-boost tools come in. They let you pay for higher placement, but only for travellers from the countries you choose.

Booking.com Visibility Booster

The Visibility Booster lets you offer extra commission on top of your standard rate in exchange for higher placement in search results. The key feature: you can target specific countries. A hotel in Kasol can say "I want to appear higher for travellers from Israel and Netherlands in October" without changing anything for Indian domestic searches.

  • Go to: Booking.com Extranet (your partner dashboard) > Opportunities > Visibility Booster
  • Set the extra commission percentage (typically 5 to 15% on top of your base commission)
  • Set the date range to match your peak international season
  • Optionally: set specific source markets (country codes like IL for Israel, NL for Netherlands, DE for Germany)

You pay the extra commission only when a booking from that market actually comes in. No booking means no extra cost. This makes it low-risk to test during your first international season.

For a full walkthrough of how the Visibility Booster works, see our dedicated guide: Booking.com Visibility Booster for Indian hotels.

Expedia TravelAds

TravelAds is Expedia's paid placement product. It works like Google Ads but inside the Expedia platform. You set a bid per click and your property appears above organic listings for your target traveller profile. You can target by origin country, travel dates, and device type.

TravelAds is managed from your Expedia Partner Central dashboard. Budget can start as low as ₹5,000 per month. On a ₹5,000 per night room rate, even 2 to 3 extra bookings per month covers the ad spend several times over.

Agoda YCS: PromoPop and Preferred Partner

Agoda's YCS portal has two tools for visibility in Southeast Asian markets.

  • PromoPop: Flash deal visibility. Your property appears in "limited-time offer" sections for travellers from Agoda's Southeast Asian markets. Good for filling last-minute gaps in occupancy.
  • Preferred Partner Programme: Pay a higher commission (20 to 22%) and get a "Preferred" badge that moves you to the top of search results for Southeast Asian traveller searches. On a ₹6,000 room, the extra 4 to 5% commission is ₹240 to ₹300 per night. If it fills otherwise-empty rooms, the maths works in your favour.

How to make your listing appeal to international travellers

Getting listed and boosted gets you seen. Converting that visibility into bookings requires your listing to speak to what international guests actually look for. These six things separate a listing that converts from one that does not.

  1. Write English content directly, not through translation. Do not take your Hindi or regional language description and run it through Google Translate. International guests notice awkward phrasing and skip. Write two clear English paragraphs from scratch. Clarity beats length every time.
  2. Photos that show what international guests care about. Room cleanliness, bathroom condition (does it have hot water? A Western-style toilet?), common areas, view from the balcony, and any unique local experience: mountain views, backwater access, fort views. International travellers book for experience, not just price.
  3. State your amenities with specifics. Not "Wi-Fi available" but "Wi-Fi speed: 50 Mbps, works in all rooms." Not "hot water" but "24-hour hot water geysers in all bathrooms." Not "front desk" but "front desk staffed 8am to 11pm; late arrivals please WhatsApp in advance." International guests arriving on late flights need to know this before they book.
  4. Use a flexible cancellation policy for international bookings. International guests book 30 to 60 days in advance and often need flexibility for visa or flight changes. A 24-hour free cancellation policy converts significantly better than a no-refund policy for this segment. Many channel managers let you set different cancellation policies per OTA, so you can keep a stricter domestic policy on MakeMyTrip and a flexible one on Expedia.
  5. Respond fast, across all time zones. Booking.com penalises properties with a message response rate below 90% in search rankings. International enquiries arrive at night (India time) because of time zone differences. Aim to respond within 4 hours to all messages. Even an automated acknowledgement helps your response rate metric.
  6. Remember your Form C obligation. All foreign nationals must have their passport details captured in Form C within 24 hours of check-in. This is a legal requirement under the Foreigners Act. Remind your front desk staff of this for every international guest. Non-compliance carries fine risk and can flag your property in local police records.

Frequently asked questions

Which OTA should I use to attract American tourists to my hotel in India?

Expedia is the primary OTA for US and Canadian travellers. A listing on Expedia Partner Central also places you on Hotels.com automatically, since both platforms are owned by Expedia Group. For US travellers, also consider activating TravelAds on Expedia with source market set to United States during the peak US travel season (October to January for India).

Is Expedia available for hotel listings in India?

Yes. Indian hotels can list directly on Expedia via Expedia Partner Central (partners.expediagroup.com). There is no restriction for Indian properties. The onboarding process takes 5 to 7 business days. Commission is typically 15 to 25% depending on your property type.

What is the Booking.com Visibility Booster and how does it help international guests find my hotel?

The Visibility Booster is a feature in your Booking.com extranet (the partner dashboard where you manage your listing) that lets you offer extra commission in exchange for higher placement in search results. The key advantage for international targeting: you can set it to activate only for travellers from specific countries. A hotel in Goa can run a Visibility Booster targeting Germany and UK during November to February without affecting their ranking for Indian domestic travellers at all.

How do I know which nationalities visit my hotel's destination?

Four sources. First, your own C-Form and GRC guest register: look at the nationality column for the past 12 months. Second, your Booking.com extranet under Analytics, then Booking by market. Third, state tourism board annual reports: all major tourism states publish nationality breakdowns. Fourth, ask other hoteliers in your local cluster. Local knowledge is often more accurate than published data.

Do I need a channel manager if I list on multiple international OTAs?

Yes, strongly recommended. When you are listed on Booking.com, Expedia, and Agoda simultaneously with separate inventory, the risk of double-bookings is high. A channel manager (software that syncs your availability across all OTAs at once) keeps all platforms updated in real time from a single dashboard. See our guide on channel managers for Indian hotels for a full breakdown.

What commission does Agoda charge Indian hotels?

Agoda typically charges 15 to 18% commission for most Indian hotel properties. On a ₹5,000 per night room rate, that is ₹750 to ₹900 per booking. If you opt into Agoda's Preferred Partner Programme, commission rises to 20 to 22% (₹1,000 to ₹1,100 per ₹5,000 booking) but you get significantly higher placement in search results for Southeast Asian markets. For properties where Agoda is a primary international channel, the Preferred programme often pays for itself in additional bookings.
