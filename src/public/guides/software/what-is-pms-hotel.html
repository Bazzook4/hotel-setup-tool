What is PMS in Hotels? Property Management System

By OnlineHotelier Insights Team 5 min read

What is PMS?

PMS = Property Management System

The central software that runs your hotel operations - reservations, check-in/out, billing, housekeeping, and reporting.

Core Engine Reservations · Front Desk · Night Audit
Ops Control Housekeeping · Maintenance
Distribution Channel Manager · OTA Sync
Guest Journey Profiles · Messaging · Upsells
Intelligence Reports · Analytics
Finance Billing · Payments · Ledgers

The PMS core handles reservations and front desk, while add-on blocks plug in operations, distribution, guest experience, analytics, and finance workflows.

What Does a PMS Do?

Function What It Does
Reservations Accept, modify, cancel bookings
Front Desk Check-in, check-out, room assignment
Room Inventory Track available, occupied, OOO rooms
Billing Create bills, post charges, process payments
Guest Profiles Store guest history, preferences, contact info
Housekeeping Room status, cleaning schedules
Reports Occupancy, revenue, ADR, guest reports
Night Audit End-of-day closing and reconciliation

Why Every Hotel Needs a PMS

  • No more manual registers: All data in one digital system
  • Real-time availability: Instantly know which rooms are free
  • Faster check-in: Guest details auto-populated from booking
  • Accurate billing: No manual calculation errors
  • Guest history: Remember returning guests and their preferences
  • Reports on demand: Know your numbers without spreadsheets
  • OTA integration: Connect to Channel Manager for auto-updates
Real Example: Without PMS, a 50-room hotel manually tracks bookings in registers, risking overbookings. With PMS, the same hotel processes 100 bookings/day with zero errors and instant availability updates.

Types of PMS

Type Description Best For
Cloud-based Access from anywhere via browser Most hotels today
On-premise Installed on local server Large chains with IT team
Mobile-first Run from tablet/smartphone Small boutique hotels

PMS vs Channel Manager vs RMS

System Primary Function
PMS Daily hotel operations (front desk, billing, housekeeping)
Channel Manager Sync rates/availability across OTAs
RMS Revenue Management - pricing recommendations
CRS Central Reservation System - multi-property bookings
Pro Tip: Many modern PMS include built-in Channel Manager. If not, ensure your PMS integrates with your preferred Channel Manager via API.

What to Look for in a PMS

  1. Ease of use: Staff should learn it in 1-2 days
  2. Cloud-based: Access from anywhere, auto-updates
  3. OTA integrations: Connect to MakeMyTrip, Booking.com, etc.
  4. GST compliance: Generate GST-compliant invoices
  5. Mobile app: Check reports on the go
  6. Support: 24/7 support in Hindi/English
  7. Reports: Occupancy, revenue, guest reports built-in
  8. Pricing: Fits your budget, no hidden fees
