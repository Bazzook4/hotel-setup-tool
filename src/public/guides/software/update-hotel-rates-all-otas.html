How to Update Rates on All OTAs at Once

One Dashboard. All Platforms. Done in Seconds.

A channel manager lets you change your room rate once and instantly pushes it to MakeMyTrip, Goibibo, Booking.com, Agoda, and every other OTA you're listed on. No separate logins. No manual errors. Most updates sync in under 60 seconds.

Why Updating Rates Manually Across OTAs Is Costing You Money

  • Most new hotels list on 4–6 OTAs — every rate change means 4–6 separate logins
  • One missed update creates rate disparity — MMT shows ₹2,500, Booking.com shows ₹3,200 for the same room
  • OTAs detect disparity and penalise you: lower search ranking, suppressed listings, loss of Genius or MMT Preferred Partner status
  • Manual updates during peak demand (long weekends, festival season) are too slow — you miss the revenue window
  • Human error is common: wrong rate, wrong date, wrong room type across 5 extranets
  • Time cost: 10 minutes per OTA, done daily, adds up to 5+ hours a week of admin work
Watch out: A rate mismatch of just ₹200 between OTAs is enough for Booking.com to flag your listing and reduce your visibility in search results.

How a Channel Manager Pushes Rates to All Platforms at Once

  • A channel manager sits between your hotel and all OTAs via live API connections
  • You set or change your rate once in one dashboard — the system broadcasts it to every connected OTA simultaneously
  • Two-way sync: OTA bookings also pull inventory back, preventing double bookings
  • Most rate updates propagate in under 60 seconds across all platforms
  • Your rates, availability, room types, and restrictions (min stay, stop sell) all sync together — not just the price

Not sure what a channel manager is? Read our complete Channel Manager guide first.

Step-by-Step: Update Your Hotel Rate on Every OTA in One Place

  1. Log into your channel manager dashboard (RezNext, Staah, Siteminder, eZee, AxisRooms, Djubo, etc.)
  2. Go to Rates & Availability — also labelled as Bulk Update, Rate Calendar, or Rate Plan in some tools
  3. Select the room type, date range, and enter your new rate
  4. Choose to apply to all connected channels, or select specific OTAs if needed
  5. Save and confirm — the system pushes the update; check the activity log to verify it was received
Pro tip: After every bulk rate update, open one OTA (e.g., Booking.com partner hub) and verify the new rate is live. Most syncs complete in 60 seconds.

Rate Parity: Why All Your OTA Prices Must Match

  • Rate parity means your room rate must be the same on every OTA — if MMT shows ₹2,800, Booking.com must also show ₹2,800
  • OTAs actively monitor each other using price-scraping tools; if they find you cheaper elsewhere, they can suppress your listing
  • A channel manager enforces parity automatically because all rates come from one source — no room for human inconsistency
  • Exception: your own hotel website can legally offer a lower "direct booking" rate in most cases — OTA parity clauses usually don't restrict your own site
  • Rate parity violations from Booking.com or MMT can trigger automatic rate matching, cutting your revenue without warning

BAR Rate and Derived Rates: The Smart Way to Price

  • BAR (Best Available Rate) is your base rack rate — the single price from which all other rates are calculated
  • Derived rates are automatically set as a percentage or fixed amount above or below BAR (e.g., CP plan = BAR + ₹400 for breakfast)
  • Change your BAR once, and every derived rate (EP, CP, MAP, AP) updates automatically across all OTAs — no manual recalculation
  • This is essential for hotels offering multiple meal plans, which are common across Indian hotels
  • Most channel managers support derived rates natively — set it up once and let the system handle the math

Common Mistakes When Syncing Hotel Rates on OTAs

  • Leaving "stop sell" switched on by accident — your room looks fully booked even when it's empty
  • Updating the rate but forgetting to update minimum length of stay (MLOS) restrictions — guests can't book even though the rate is live
  • Pushing rates without syncing inventory — rate shows ₹2,500 but 0 rooms available, so the listing is invisible
  • Ignoring the activity or audit log — failed syncs go unnoticed for hours without a daily check
  • Using different rate plan names across OTAs — makes parity tracking almost impossible
  • Over-relying on the channel manager without doing a manual spot-check on one OTA after any bulk update

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I update rates on MakeMyTrip and Goibibo at the same time?

Yes — if both are connected to your channel manager, one rate update reaches both platforms simultaneously within seconds.

What happens if the channel manager sync fails?

The rate on that OTA stays at the old price until you manually update it or re-trigger the sync. Check your activity log daily to catch failed syncs early.

Do I need a channel manager if I only list on 2 OTAs?

At 2 OTAs it's optional, but from 3 OTAs onwards a channel manager saves significant time and eliminates rate parity risks that can hurt your rankings.

Will my hotel website booking rate update too?

Only if your direct booking engine is integrated with the same channel manager. Most modern setups include this — check with your provider.

How long does a rate update take to appear on all OTAs?

Most channel managers sync rate updates across all connected OTAs in under 60 seconds, though some OTAs can take up to 5 minutes to reflect changes on their live pages.

Which channel manager is best for small hotels in India?

For small hotels (under 20 rooms), AxisRooms, Staah, and eZee Centrix are popular affordable options starting around ₹6,000–₹12,000/year. Always check which OTAs each supports before signing up.
