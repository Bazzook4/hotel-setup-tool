Why Your Hotel Needs Its Own Website

OTAs bring bookings, but they extract a steep price. Every booking through MakeMyTrip or Booking.com costs you 15–18% in commission. A direct booking from your own website costs you nothing. Here is what you gain the day your site goes live.

₹

Zero Commission on Direct Bookings

A hotel with 10 rooms at ₹5,000 ADR and 65% occupancy loses roughly ₹17 lakh a year to OTA commission. Even 25% direct bookings cuts that by ₹4.3 lakh annually.

📋

You Own the Guest Relationship

OTAs never give you the guest's phone number or email. With direct bookings, you build your own guest database for upsells, return visits and direct re-marketing.

🔍

Rank on Google for Your Location

A properly built website ranks for searches like "boutique hotel in Jaipur" or "beach resort Goa". OTA listings never rank for your specific property name the way your own site does.

🏷️

Offer Direct-Only Rates

OTA rate parity clauses are being relaxed. You can now offer guests a small direct discount — or include extras like early check-in — to nudge them away from OTAs.

🧾

GST Invoices Under Your GSTIN

Direct bookings let you issue GST invoices under your own registration. Corporate guests get clean input tax credit. OTA-routed bookings complicate this chain.

📱

WhatsApp Bookings from Your Site

Indian travellers prefer WhatsApp to fill out forms. A floating WhatsApp button on your site converts browsing into a conversation — and conversations into confirmed bookings.

Heritage Theme: For Boutique and Heritage Hotels

The Heritage theme works for properties where the physical space is the product — havelis in Jaisalmer, plantation bungalows in Coorg, tea estate homes in Munnar, old colonial properties in Pondicherry. Guests are buying an experience, not a commodity room.

Best for: Palace hotels and havelis, heritage homestays in Rajasthan, Kerala and Himachal Pradesh, boutique jungle lodges, colonial-era properties. Average Daily Rate typically ₹4,000–₹15,000.

The Heritage theme emphasises storytelling. It gives you full-bleed photography sections, a property history timeline, and an experiences block — treks, cooking classes, heritage walks — all designed to justify a premium price over budget alternatives on OTAs.

Business Theme: For City and Highway Hotels

The Business theme is the most stripped-back and conversion-focused of the three. It is built for properties in Bengaluru tech corridors, Mumbai business districts, Pune IT parks, Delhi NCR corporate zones and national highway properties. The guest knows what they need. The website's job is to confirm it is available and make booking frictionless.

Best for: City business hotels, corporate service apartments, highway hotels, airport properties. Average Daily Rate typically ₹3,500–₹8,000.

Corporate accounts are a major revenue line for business hotels. The Business theme includes a dedicated corporate rate enquiry form so companies can negotiate a rate and book on account — something OTAs cannot facilitate cleanly. The sticky Book Now button stays visible as the guest scrolls through room options, removing the need to hunt for a booking path.

What is a booking engine? It is software embedded in your website that lets guests check real-time availability, select a room type, and pay — without leaving your site, and without going through an OTA. You collect the payment directly and keep 100% of the room revenue.
